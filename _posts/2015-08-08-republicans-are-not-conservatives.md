It's hard to be a conservative. Firstly, most people have no idea what is means to be a conservative. Let me serve up an anecdote: when asked if George Bush is a conservative, William Buckley replied, “He's conservative, not a conservative.” There is a bright difference between someone who behaves conservatively and someone who is a intellectual and/or political conservative. The the currently political climate as it is, confrontation and divisive, these distinctions are not being made and it is for the worse.

A good friend of mine once said, “Liberalism and conservatism both derive from the same Western intellectual and political traditions. They are two sides to the same coin.” I believe this to be absolutely true. A sincere conservative does not want anything different than a liberal. While too much government is undesirable, government remains, nonetheless, absolutely essential. Real conservatives should champion the cause of prudence, which suggests the proper and good governance are the driving principals of their politics. Idealism must be left behind in politics, as politics is the realm of reality and ideals have no place there.![Edmund Burke]({{ site.url }}/images/burke_message.jpg){: .image-pull-right} Edmund Burke, arguably the father of the conservative intellectual movement, points readily to the French Revolution – a case in point of ideals run amok – to prove this point.

The Republican Party no longer represents conservatism, but rather reactionary ideas. While conservatives and reactionaries are said to be on the same side of the political spectrum, to my mind they could not be further apart. A conservative believes that we should strive for universal health care, but doesn't trust the federal government to implement it, rather the solution should have been on the state level. Republicans want to abolish the ACA, but never stop to consider why nor what would replace it. This comes off as simply callous. Government should serve and help its people, not do them harm. You cannot give people health care then take it away; that is reactionary, not conservative. A conservative would accept the law, and strive to improve upon it, not rip it down.

While I may personally err on the side of “pro-life”, I have absolutely no intention of ever wanting to change Roe v. Wade. No conservative should. Once again, the call to repeal Roe v. Wade represents a reactionary response, which is the message being propagated by the Republican party. In you read Burke's reflection, he shows that once you give people a right, to take it away only leads to chaos. Thus is the cause of conservatism, to limit the granting of “rights” until society is ready for them. Society accepts and is ready for abortion rights. Society accepts and is ready for gay-marriage rights. Society accepts and is ready for universal health care. These are causes that have stood the trials of prudence and have entered into our tradition and should be embraced by conservatives everywhere, not rejected.

Republicans want to lower the deficit, a worth cause, but aren't willing to raise taxes, as prudence would dictate. That is simply good governance. They want to reduce welfare, but aren't willing to raise the minimum wage to encourage less people to rely on entitlements. The reject the premise of climate change, but aren't willing to reduce the egregious amounts of CO2 pollution which no one denies exists. Republicans support of Citizens United, which represents the complete and utter degradation of our electoral process and amounts to allowing wealthy individuals to own politicians. In his life time, Edmund Burke fought vigorously to cleanse British Parliament of this very plague, notably in his battles against the East India Company. Ideals are trumping prudence in politics, and the results are messy: schools are being defended; infrastructure is crumbling; we're funding our military as if we've going to fight a war as if it were the 20th century, event though that era has long past us by; the government is invading our homes with mass surveillance , and yet we're allowing it in the name of security; government has even shut down under Republican leadership, which should represent the ultimate sin to conservatives who champion prudence.

It is clear that the Republican party cannot and will not represent true conservatives, but I fear that a home for those that think as I do no longer exists. We're forced to sit on the sidelines and root for those politicians that we believe will govern correctly, and we are willing to bend our personal political beliefs in the name of good government.
